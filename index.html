The 5 moves — in this order

  1. Lead with THEIR number — not your price
    “How much funding are you going after, and when's the decision?”
    Write it down. Every number after this anchors to it.
  2. Reframe: proof, not promises
    “Committees fund proof, not slides. Let me give you a live product they can touch.”
  3. Let the demo close it
    Hand them a phone → “Enroll a farmer.” Then open the dashboard → “See the count jump? Districts, women farmers, acres — live. One click exports it for your grant report.”
  4. Stack the value, THEN say the price
    “Built properly this is 1.35 lakh of work. Because it's a focused demo for your pitch — ₹60,000. That's about ___% of the ₹___ you're going after.”
  5. Ask for the start — then go silent
    “Half now, half on delivery, unlimited revisions till you're proud of it. Any reason we wouldn't start today?”
    Then STOP. The next person who speaks loses.

If they push back

Can you do it cheaper?
Don't cut price — split terms: “₹30k to start today, ₹30k on delivery.”
Send me the details, I'll think about it.
“Sure — when's your pitch date? Let's lock the next step now and work back from it.”
I need committee / approval.
“Makes sense — who's in the room and when? I'll give you a one-pager to take in.”
Why not just a Google Form?
“A form collects names. This gives the committee a live product + a dashboard to fund.”
“A live demo + impact dashboard, ready [X] days before your pitch — for ₹60k. Any reason we wouldn't start today?”
↳ Then STOP TALKING. Silence does the closing.

Do

  • Call it a “funding-ready enrollment system”
  • Demo on a real phone, in their language
  • Name a deadline tied to their pitch
  • Ask for 50% to book the slot

Don't

  • Say “website” (kills the price)
  • Quote before they say their funding number
  • Discount — change the terms instead
  • Promise you'll win them the grant
Terms: 50% now, 50% on delivery · unlimited revisions · live before their deadline. Plant the upsell: “When the grant lands, the full platform + retainer is the natural next step — and I'm already your guy.”
